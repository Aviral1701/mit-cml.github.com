Joined by other representatives from the MIT App Inventor team and Phil Puthumana of the <a href="http://www.verizonfoundation.org/ ">Verizon Foundation</a>, we gathered to prepare to train the ten Best in Nation winning teams who competed in the <a href="http://verizonfoundation.org/appchallenge/">Verizon Innovative App Challenge</a>.</p> <p>These winning teams, <a href="http://newscenter.verizon.com/corporate/news-articles/2013-03-18-innovative-app-challenge/">announced on Monday afternoon</a>, were selected through a rigorous judging process from a pool of more than 470 submitted concepts. Each winning team has been matched with an MIT App Inventor Training Corps member, who will provide online and in person support as the teams use App Inventor as a tool to refine and build the apps they proposed in their Challenge proposals.</p> <p>We're very fortunate to have such experts as part of this initial group of trainers, and look forward to witnessing the work they do in concert with the winners of the Verizon Innovative App Challenge. </p> <p>We're also fortunate to be working with an amazing team from the Verizon Foundation.
