Our First APP OF THE MONTH! http://explore.appinventor.mit.edu/blogs/ruff/2014/05-0 <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden view-mode-rss"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><p> </p><h3>Congratulations to CitizenReport!</h3> <p>This emergency preparedness app has been selected as our first ever App Inventor App of the Month! CitizenReport is representing our Apps for Communities category, one of four for which we are accepting submissions. Read on to find out more about this award-winning app!</p> <p></p> <div id="container" style="width:79%;margin:auto;float:right;"> <b>CitizenReport</b> | <i>Apps for Communities Category</i><br /><br /><b>What the app does</b><br /> CitizenReport allows a community of prescreened volunteered to report incidents in their locations to emergency personnel and first responders. Volunteers take and submit pictures of incidents tagged with a specific topic (e.g., damaged buildings). They can subscribe to these tags to received updates from other volunteers and first responders who are deployed via national or local incident management systems.<br /><br /><b>Why it was built</b><br /> In the aftermath of the crises that surrounded the Boston Marathon in April 2013, the creators saw a need to connect the power of the volunteer community to first responders to share information in emergency situations.<br /><br /><b>Who built it</b><br /> Weihua Li, senior in college, and Oshani Seneviratne, Ph.D. student<br /><br /><b>App Status</b><br /> The creators built and submitted this pair of apps in the AT&amp;T Hackathon at MIT in May 2014. They selected App Inventor to develop the apps because they only 24 hours to design and program their app and wanted a tool that could facilitate quick development. They won the “Best Communication App” prize in the “Emergency Preparedness” category.<br /><br /><i>Do you have an App Inventor app you think should be App of the Week?
