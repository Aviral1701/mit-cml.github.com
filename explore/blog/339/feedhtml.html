Read on to find out more.</p> <p></p> <div id="container" style="width:79%;margin:auto;float:right;"> <b>One Percent Fitness</b> | <i>Apps for Individuals Category</i><br /><br /><b>What the app does</b><br /> One Percent Fitness tracks up to six of your fitness goals (running, push-ups, etc.). When a user logs a workout, the app sets a goal that is 1% higher for their next workout. For example, if a user run 5 miles today, the app logs his workout and sets his goal for his next run at 5.05 miles. This helps users gradually increase the intensity of their workouts. Users can also set goals related to time, repetitions, and weight used.<br /><br /><b>Why it was built</b><br /> This started with creator Jared Carey’s personal goal to find the most efficient workout possible. He began by using a rowing machine but struggled to build endurance. He decided to start small and aim for 1% longer durations with every workout. Jared built a basic app to track this goal. The first version had four simple text boxes, some math, and a TinyDB to track his rowing progress. As his rowing workouts improved (from 10 minutes to 26+ minutes on the rowing machine!), he decided to expand his fitness regime and his app. He started over with App Inventor 2. His goal was to build an app with a clean user interface and release it on the Play Store for all to use. (No ads, no accounts, no personal data shared, minimal permissions, no internet or GPS or other sensors required.)<br /><br /><b>Who built it</b><br /> Jared Carey, <i>31</i>, GIS Specialist<br /><br /><b>App Status</b><br /> This app is available on the Play Store, ready to download in all English-speaking countries. This is a beta version. Jared is open to suggestions on how to make it better.<br /><br /><i>Do you have an App Inventor app you think should be App of the Week? CitizenReport is representing our Apps for Communities category, one of four for which we are accepting submissions. Read on to find out more about this award-winning app!</p> <p></p> <div id="container" style="width:79%;margin:auto;float:right;"> <b>CitizenReport</b> | <i>Apps for Communities Category</i><br /><br /><b>What the app does</b><br /> CitizenReport allows a community of prescreened volunteered to report incidents in their locations to emergency personnel and first responders. Volunteers take and submit pictures of incidents tagged with a specific topic (e.g., damaged buildings). They can subscribe to these tags to received updates from other volunteers and first responders who are deployed via national or local incident management systems.<br /><br /><b>Why it was built</b><br /> In the aftermath of the crises that surrounded the Boston Marathon in April 2013, the creators saw a need to connect the power of the volunteer community to first responders to share information in emergency situations.<br /><br /><b>Who built it</b><br /> Weihua Li, senior in college, and Oshani Seneviratne, Ph.D. student<br /><br /><b>App Status</b><br /> The creators built and submitted this pair of apps in the AT&amp;T Hackathon at MIT in May 2014. They selected App Inventor to develop the apps because they only 24 hours to design and program their app and wanted a tool that could facilitate quick development.
